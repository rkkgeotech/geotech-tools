Active Pressure (§3.11.5.3): Use Rankine for long-heel cantilever walls where the failure plane clears the wall stem (soil-on-soil). Use Coulomb for gravity, semigravity, prefab modular, MSE, and short-heel cantilever walls where wall geometry intercepts the failure plane.

Passive Pressure (§3.11.5.4) — NAVFAC DM-7.2 / Kerisel & Absi (1990): The figures use a two-step workflow: (1) read k′p from the primary curve for your wall/backfill geometry (curves are drawn at full wall friction, δ/φf = −1), then (2) multiply by reduction factor R from the table for your actual δ/φf ratio. The result is kₚ = k′p × R. δ/φf = 0 gives exact Rankine.
Fig. 3.11.5.4-1 Vertical wall (θ=90°), horizontal backfill k′p from primary curve × R from reduction table
Fig. 3.11.5.4-2 Vertical wall, sloping backfill — β/φ ratio k′p = kRankine-sloped(φ,β) / R(φ,0)  × R(φ,−δ/φ)

Equivalent-Fluid Method (§3.11.5.5): Applicable only where Rankine theory is valid and backfill is free-draining granular soil (<5% passing No. 200). Wall height ≤ 20 ft. Active values assume Δ/H = 1/240 lateral movement. Minimum γeq = 0.030 kcf per AASHTO. For sloping backfill, a vertical force component also acts: Pv = Ph · tan(β).

Bearing Resistance Slope Reduction Coefficient, RCBC (§10.6.3.1.2c): Table 10.6.3.1.2c-2 — Footings placed adjacent to slopes. RCBC is applied to the nominal bearing resistance: qn,slope = RCBC × qn,flat. RCBC = 1.0 means no reduction; RCBC = 0 means no bearing capacity. Stability number Ns = γH/c′. For purely cohesionless soils (c′=0), select the c′=0 column. Linear interpolation is permitted for intermediate values per AASHTO.
