Field Procedure (Wolman 1954)
1
Select the reach. For stream characterization, sample pools and riffles at the same proportion they occur in the reach.
2
Start at a random point. Throw a pebble along the stream edge to pick the starting transect location. Take one step into the water perpendicular to flow.
3
Blind selection. Avert your eyes, then pick up the first pebble touching your index finger next to your big toe. Do not visually select particles.
4
Measure the b-axis (intermediate / width axis). Use a gravelometer — record which hole the pebble fits through. For embedded or immovable pebbles, measure the shortest visible axis.
5
Step and repeat. Take another step across the stream and repeat until you reach the opposite bank. Establish a new transect and begin again. For narrow reaches (<2 m), walk upstream in a zig-zag pattern.
6
Collect ≥ 100 measurements per location to accurately quantify particle size distributions. Plot data on a log₂ scale.
SHA Note (App. 14-B): The D50 from channel bed material at the bridge section (Locs 4–6) is used directly in ABSCOUR 9 clear-water scour equations. The upstream riffle sample (Loc 2) informs the live-bed vs. clear-water scour determination. Maryland stream morphology studies suggest clear-water scour conditions predominate in many non-tidal streams.
